about: 'This project delivers a zero-cost, instant respiratory and cardiac early warning
  check accessible to anyone with a smartphone. Using only the device microphone,
  the web app guides users through a short, clinically inspired assessment—breathing,
  coughing, and speech tasks—and applies lightweight AI to detect patterns associated
  with conditions such as Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease, asthma, and early
  respiratory infections. In under two minutes, users receive a simple risk indication
  (green/amber/red), empowering them to seek care earlier, reduce uncertainty, and
  avoid deterioration before symptoms become critical. The solution is fully browser-based,
  requires no download, and works on any modern phone—making it inherently scalable
  across the ~3 million population served by NHS Wales.


  The impact is immediate and system-wide: by shifting detection earlier in the pathway,
  this tool has the potential to reduce avoidable GP visits, prevent emergency admissions,
  and support proactive self-management—particularly for high-risk and underserved
  populations. It complements existing NHS services by acting as a front-door triage
  layer, filtering low-risk cases while flagging those needing urgent attention. Built
  with privacy in mind (audio processed transiently, no long-term storage), and deployable
  at near-zero marginal cost, the platform demonstrates how AI can be responsibly
  embedded into everyday care. With further validation, it could integrate into NHS
  digital pathways, community outreach, and remote monitoring programmes—turning any
  smartphone into a scalable, equitable screening tool for respiratory health.'
