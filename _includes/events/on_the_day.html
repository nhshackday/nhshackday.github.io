Who should come to NHS Hack Days?

If you’re from a background which isn’t well-represented in most geeky meetups, come along – we want to make a difference.

If you’re from a background which is well-represented in most geeky meetups, come along – we want your help to make a difference.

If you’re worried about not being computery enough, come.

If you think you’re an imposter, come.

If your day job isn’t code, come.

This isn’t a group of experts, just people. We are interested in the social and technical problems. This is a support group. No-one knows what they are doing.

Pitch Perfect

The pitch is the basis of every successful project. Developers are looking to evaluate a pitch on 2 basic criteria:

  • Can it be done in a weekend?

    The judging criteria requires all projects to be up and running for people to use.

  • Will it provde real benefit?

    Hack days are the perfect way to create simple solutions that can save the nhs time and money. The initial focus should start with a user need

With that in mind there are 2 common pitfalls

  • Does it involve patient data?

    understandably there are lot legal, technical and moral issues around patient data. It is probably better to avoid this minefield

  • Has it already been done before?

    This is really common. There are a lot of people trying to create utitilities to get things done have a look around and make sure you're proposing something unique.

Find a group

Groups form quite organically, and some people join several groups rather than committing to just one project. You may choose to join a particular group because you were inspired by the pitch, or because you have skills you think might be useful, or for any reason at all. Sometimes groups end up merging depending on how the hacking goes. It's all very freeform as you might have gathered.

Guidelines for presentations

  • Strict time limit of 3-4 mins (tbc) depending on numbers
  • Please submit slides in advance with the form above. You should give us the URL of a public web-accessible presentation (using something like Prezi or Google Presentation) or the URL of a pdf containing your slides.
  • You can demo your project live if required, but only by navigating to a public web address using the machine already set up with our AV equipment; basically, you can't plug in your own machine to our AV equipment. This is for the sake of common sense, time limits and everyone's sanity.

Guidelines for projects

  • Submissions should offer concrete contributions, by example, to the question: How can we build an environment where world class NHS digital services flourish?
  • It must be possible for someone to get some benefit out of the submission tomorrow e.g. A usable but buggy system is fine, a set of photoshop layouts of a system is not.
  • It was done over the weekend. If you're adding additional functionality to an existing service only the additional functionality will be considered
Extra credit
  • something that can straight away be used in the field.
  • Something that could change Government policy simply by existing.
